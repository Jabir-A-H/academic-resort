Course Title:

Management & Organizational Behavior

Credit Hours:

3 Credits

Semester:

2nd Semester

Prerequisites:

Introduction to Business (1102)

This course explores the fundamental principles of management and organizational behavior, covering leadership styles, team dynamics, organizational culture, motivation theories, and strategic planning. Students will develop skills in people management, decision-making, and organizational analysis.

‚ùì Question Banks & Exams

Mid-term Examinations

Sample Mid-term Questions

Q1: Compare and contrast the four major functions of management (planning, organizing, leading, controlling). Provide examples of each function in a business context.

Q2: Explain Maslow's hierarchy of needs theory. How can managers use this theory to motivate employees in the workplace?

Q3: Describe the different leadership styles (autocratic, democratic, laissez-faire). When would each style be most appropriate?

Q4: What are the characteristics of effective teams? Discuss the stages of team development according to Tuckman's model.

Final Examinations

Sample Final Questions

Q1: Analyze the impact of organizational culture on employee behavior. How can managers shape and change organizational culture?

Q2: Discuss the concept of organizational change. What are the common sources of resistance to change and how can they be overcome?

Q3: Evaluate different decision-making models used in organizations. How does group decision-making differ from individual decision-making?

Q4: Examine the role of communication in organizational effectiveness. What are the barriers to effective communication and how can they be minimized?
