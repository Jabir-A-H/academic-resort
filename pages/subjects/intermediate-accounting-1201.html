📋 Course Overview

Course Code:

1201

Course Title:

Intermediate Accounting

Credit Hours:

3 Credits

Semester:

2nd Semester

Prerequisites:

Introduction to Financial Accounting (1101)

This course builds upon foundational accounting principles, focusing on advanced financial accounting concepts, complex transactions, and comprehensive financial reporting. Students will learn advanced revenue recognition, asset valuation, liability management, and equity accounting principles essential for professional accounting practice.

❓ Question Banks & Exams

Mid-term Examinations

Sample Mid-term Questions

Q1: Explain the concept of revenue recognition and discuss the five-step model under IFRS 15. Provide examples of each step.

Q2: Calculate depreciation using straight-line, declining balance, and units-of-production methods for an asset costing $100,000 with a useful life of 5 years and salvage value of $10,000.

Q3: Prepare journal entries for the following transactions: (a) Purchase of equipment on credit, (b) Accrual of interest expense, (c) Prepayment of insurance.

Q4: Discuss the difference between operating and finance leases. How would each type of lease be recorded by the lessee?

Final Examinations

Sample Final Questions

Q1: Prepare a complete set of financial statements (Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement) from the given trial balance.

Q2: Analyze the financial performance of XYZ Company using liquidity, profitability, and leverage ratios. Interpret your findings.

Q3: Explain the accounting treatment for contingent liabilities and provisions. When should they be recognized, disclosed, or not reported?

Q4: Calculate the present value of future cash flows for a bond investment and determine whether it should be purchased at the current market price.
