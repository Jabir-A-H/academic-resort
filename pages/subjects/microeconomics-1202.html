Microeconomics

Semester:

Prerequisites:

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to microeconomic theory, focusing on individual economic units such as consumers, firms, and markets. Students will explore supply and demand analysis, consumer choice theory, production and cost analysis, market structures, and welfare economics.

‚ùì Question Banks & Exams

Mid-term Examinations

Sample Mid-term Questions

Q1: Define elasticity of demand and calculate the price elasticity when price increases from $10 to $12 and quantity demanded falls from 100 to 80 units.

Q2: Explain the law of diminishing marginal utility with a numerical example. How does this concept relate to the downward-sloping demand curve?

Q3: Draw and explain the market equilibrium. What happens to equilibrium price and quantity when demand increases while supply remains constant?

Q4: Compare and contrast normal goods, inferior goods, and Giffen goods. Provide real-world examples of each.

Final Examinations

Sample Final Questions

Q1: Analyze the characteristics of perfect competition, monopoly, monopolistic competition, and oligopoly. Compare their efficiency outcomes.

Q2: A firm has the production function Q = 10L^0.5 K^0.3. Calculate the marginal product of labor and capital when L=16 and K=8.

Q3: Explain the concept of consumer surplus and producer surplus. How do taxes affect these surpluses and overall market efficiency?

Q4: Discuss the theory of the firm. How does a profit-maximizing firm determine its optimal level of output in the short run?
