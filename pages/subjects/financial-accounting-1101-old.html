Course Overview

Course Code: 1101

Course Title: Introduction to Financial Accounting

Semester: 1st Semester

Prerequisites: None

This course provides a foundation in financial accounting principles, covering basic accounting concepts, financial statements preparation, and fundamental accounting procedures essential for business students.

        Mid-term Examinations

        Sample Mid-term Questions

        Q1: Define accounting and explain its importance in business decision-making.

        Q2: Prepare a trial balance from the following ledger balances and identify any errors.

        Q3: Distinguish between cash basis and accrual basis of accounting with examples.

        Q4: Calculate the missing figure in the following accounting equation: Assets = 50,000, Liabilities = ?, Owner's Equity = 30,000

        Final Examinations

        Sample Final Questions

        Q1: Prepare a complete set of financial statements (Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement) from the given trial balance.

        Q2: Explain the accounting cycle and describe each step with examples.

        Q3: Calculate depreciation using straight-line, double-declining balance, and units of production methods.

        Q4: Record journal entries for the following transactions and post them to the ledger accounts.
