🇧🇩 Course Overview

Course Code:

1205

Course Title:

Bangladesh Studies

Credit Hours:

3 Credits

Semester:

2nd Semester

Prerequisites:

None

This course provides a comprehensive understanding of Bangladesh's history, culture, political system, and socio-economic development. Students will explore the liberation war, constitutional framework, economic policies, social challenges, and Bangladesh's role in the global community.

✏️ Assignments & Homework

Assignment 1: Liberation War Analysis

Research and analyze key events and figures of the 1971 Liberation War

Assignment 2: Economic Development Review

Examine Bangladesh's economic growth and development challenges since independence

Assignment 3: Cultural Heritage Study

Explore Bangladesh's cultural diversity, traditions, and modern cultural expressions

❓ Question Banks & Exams

Mid-term Examinations

Sample Mid-term Questions

Q1: Discuss the major causes and consequences of the Language Movement of 1952. How did it contribute to the eventual independence of Bangladesh?

Q2: Analyze the role of Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman in the independence movement. Discuss his Six-Point Program and its significance.

Q3: Explain the constitutional framework of Bangladesh. What are the fundamental principles and basic features of the Constitution?

Q4: Describe the geographical features of Bangladesh. How do geography and climate affect the country's economy and culture?

Final Examinations

Sample Final Questions

Q1: Evaluate Bangladesh's economic development since independence. Discuss the challenges and achievements in key sectors like agriculture, industry, and services.

Q2: Analyze the social challenges facing Bangladesh today, including poverty, education, healthcare, and gender equality. What are the government's responses to these challenges?

Q3: Discuss Bangladesh's foreign policy and international relations. How has the country positioned itself in regional and global affairs?

Q4: Examine the cultural heritage of Bangladesh. How do literature, music, arts, and festivals reflect the national identity?
