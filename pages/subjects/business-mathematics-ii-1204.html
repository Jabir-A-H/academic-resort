📊 Course Overview

Course Code:

1204

Course Title:

Mathematics for Business Decisions-II

Credit Hours:

3 Credits

Semester:

2nd Semester

Prerequisites:

Mathematics for Business Decisions-I (1104)

This advanced mathematics course builds upon foundational concepts, focusing on calculus applications, optimization techniques, financial mathematics, and advanced statistical methods. Students will learn to apply mathematical tools to solve complex business problems and make data-driven decisions.

❓ Question Banks & Exams

Mid-term Examinations

Sample Mid-term Questions

Q1: Find the derivative of f(x) = 3x³ - 5x² + 2x - 7 and determine the critical points.

Q2: A company's total cost function is C(x) = 100 + 50x + 0.1x². Find the marginal cost when x = 200 units.

Q3: Calculate the maximum profit if the revenue function is R(x) = 80x - 0.2x² and the cost function is C(x) = 20x + 1000.

Q4: Evaluate the definite integral ∫₀⁵ (2x + 3) dx and interpret its meaning in a business context.

Final Examinations

Sample Final Questions

Q1: Solve the linear programming problem: Maximize Z = 3x + 2y subject to x + y ≤ 4, 2x + y ≤ 6, x ≥ 0, y ≥ 0.

Q2: Calculate the present value of an annuity of $5,000 per year for 10 years at an interest rate of 6% compounded annually.

Q3: A business invests $10,000 at 8% interest compounded quarterly. What will be the amount after 5 years?

Q4: Use the method of Lagrange multipliers to optimize f(x,y) = xy subject to the constraint x + y = 10.
